However, if you plan on playing with a keyboard+mouse combo, things sometimes get quite annoying where you need some precision and you have to rely on some chance factor to make it happen. Well, maybe a dose of randomness is really needed to prevent a puzzle game from being too straight forward; but I have to admit that apart from the puzzle itself, I don’t like additional things getting in my way. Cubicity often has alternative solutions -or fun ways for a better word- to solve its puzzles and they are pretty clever but still needs some additional touches to here and there to make it more appealing.</p> <p><!-- more --></p> <p>The game has no issues with its static -or animated- visual elements and audio. Actually, you might like the audio. It has become a tradition for many puzzle games to use short loops which become unbearable after a certain time but Cubicity’s sound is not annoying and rather goes pretty well with the overall atmosphere. Of course they are relatively short loops and are not heart-touching works of fine art but you get the idea -this means you don’t have to turn the audio off to continue enjoying the game.-. Graphics are fine and can scale up nicely to any high resolution monitor. You only have a windowed/fullscreen toggle though; so if you want to set a specific value for either windowed or fullscreen modes, you won’t be able to. However going this technical with Cubicity would be an overkill. If you force your luck, you will eventually cause graphic glitches in the form of making some objects vanish or get stuck yourself. It’s not that game breaking as you have to intentionally try to make these happen and if you manage to get stuck, you actually earn an achievement. Yes, Cubicity has achievements. They won’t be unlocking additional content -secret levels, alternate character/costume, etc.- so you have to have an intrinsic motivation to earn all of them.</p> <p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="313" data-orig-width="500"><img alt="cubicity" src="https://64.media.tumblr.com/fa4363f5175f977601ee8191d7687664/4fba80c04c084f3c-14/s540x810/659b9e8c9320a52c01d87364800c69920dabc055.jpg" data-orig-height="313" data-orig-width="500"/></figure></p> <blockquote> <p>Your tools display at the upper left corner shows up when you cycle through them, like Half Life.</p> </blockquote> <p>As you see in the screenshots, you are Seamus -*ahem* well, this info is from the game’s description- and hanging from the ceiling, trying to place the dark cube -which is sometimes replaced by a wooden crate- inside the box that is situated in places throughout various levels. Although this can be considered as a challenge by some; I think in puzzle-centric games, as soon as the puzzle is solved in the mind, the game should let you carry out the solution with relative ease.
